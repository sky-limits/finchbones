resting up

Sicil accepts that the stallion doesn't want her to simply stand around and talk to him - tired, and pink like a rose, he’s probably too sunburnt to think. Instead, she bows her legs and lays down next to him, grabbing some nearby moss, now soaked with the twilight dew.

Offering it to him, her mouth full, she nods at his sunburns. “Put ‘is on ‘em,” is her muffled plea, and laying them next to the white stallions legs, she beckons Cinder. “Come along, little one. It's past time we rest.” Turning her head back to the stallion, Sicil smiles and scoots herself a comfortable - but not far distance from the stallion, and Cinder lays alongside her flank.

“I'm very sorry to interrupt your rest, sir,” is how she starts, “I know you must be quite tired from the day. But the plant is cold; it'll help your sunburn if you put it on your nose.”

The stallion does so, sighing with relief when the coolness of the moss meets his burnt skin.

“I'm sorry I can't do more,” Sicil apologizes, “I don't know all of the herbs around here. However,” musing, she flicks her ears at the forest, listening to the rustling of trees. “If you came along with me and the little one here, we could help keep you safe? In the shade? Plus, I know there must be a herb in the woods for those burns; they just don't grow near the shore.”

Sicil huffs tiredly, and then smiles at the stallion. “But, I know you're tired, and my speech probably isn't helping anything. My offer - to join my herd, just me and Cinder here - to help you find something for your nose is open, all you have to do is say something.”

Then lowering her head, Sicil watches Cinder with interest; the little foal pricks her ears and swivels them like a bat, but her adoptive mother can see that the foal has begun to take long, sleepy blinks. “Shall we go bed down for the night?” Sicil asks softly, nibbling a burr out of Cinder's mane. “That way we have lots of energy for fun tomorrow, right?”

Cinder thought this over for a moment, little tail flicking from side to side. “No…” she pouted, “I don't want to sleep yet! We've barely gotten to have any fun!!”

Blowing air from her nose gently, Sicil chuckles and pulls Cinder closer into her body, sending a last friendly, open look to the stallion. As Cinder falls asleep at the mare's side, Social opens her mouth to murmur one thing more: “I hope you don't mind us spending the night here,” and tucking her tail over the foal, finishes her sentence. “Maybe we can help you, and you can join us, after we all get some rest.”
