polmeanes' storm

Vultuulf stretched her limbs, shaking out the long, webbed paws at the end of her legs.
Rain had been battering her and her companions since the dawn, and the white stripe trailing
down her back acted like a lightning strike of attention - it seemed like most of the rain fell on
her, and only her.
She shook her head, creating her own private rain shower, and listened to the
instructions being given by the lead rexal. Three teams would head out: the strongest to pull
the ships further inland, away from the fog; cunning to retrieve lost inventory and make it back
to shore safely, and fast, fast enough to swim through ripping currents and guide ships and
their passengers to shore.
Vultuulf called out when the team being chosen to pull ships was mentioned. I'm strong,
she mused, following a squadron to the ships and grabbing one of the ropes.
The rain and wind didn't make it any easier to see what she was doing, as she tied the
rope neatly in front of her shoulders, tugging on the knot and hoping it was secure - hoping
she wouldn't trip and stumble into the muddy shores of the island while trying to help.
The fog had been encroaching fast when she and her fellow Dome dwellers were called
to the Posian Islands, to assist them in their plight. Much help was needed as the storm
developed, and the thoughts brewed in Vultuulf 's mind like the storm clouds on the horizon.
Muscles straining, she pulled the ship forward, away from the dangerously soupy fog. This fog
had been here before she was born, and it would continue to be. Another step in the sand,
leading her forward, sharp claws digging in for purchase, steps syncing to her fellows.
This fog threatened the islands - untie yourself, dash to the next ship, a great and
mighty beast, nearly swallowed by the fog, tie yourself and pull - and the livelihoods of the
rexes on them.
The third ship is the one to get Vultuulf, bringing her blue shoulders crashing into the
sand as her rope suddenly loosens. Spitting the gritty grains of sand from between her gums,
she stands again, and grips the frayed end of the rope between her teeth this time, closing her
eyes against the strain it creates and pulling with all her might.
Others around her fell in similar measure and time, like an invisible hand keeping tempo
to sweep them all over. In reality, the storm was worsening, and it was nigh impossible to defeat
the deafening scream of the wind - even the rain that battered down on scales and fur alike was
louder than the rushing blood in ones’ ears.
The fog crept ever closer, seeping in like water under a door you thought was sealed
tight. It was a malevolent entity, omnipresent, neverending, and it seemed like it got closer to
the shore the more ships Vultuulf and her team pulled to the shore.
Further down, towards already emptied shells of buildings, the fog curled and snuck, a
lithe claw of it hoping to latch onto the furthest ship. Her masts were in danger of the fog
touching them, but so far she rocked in the water unscathed. Someone on Vultuulf ’s team
called out, a thing near to a scream, and they ran to the ship, tying themselves with haste.
As they pulled, the fog pulled harder, and touched the very edge of the wooden
planking. Vultuulf watched as puller after puller bit through or unraveled themselves from the
rope otherwise, dashing away as the fog seemed to climb up even faster around the ship’s body,
ever hungrier as it had finally found something to consume.
Vultuulf nosed her way out of the rope that bound her to the ship, turning away from
the doomed ship. There would be nothing left to save now - she just had to hope it was
abandoned, like the town the fog was rolling towards.
She could do nothing but go to the next ship, pull it onto the sand, and then the
next…and the next…and the next. Eventually, the ships seemed safer than they had before, or at
least safe enough to deem leaving for the time being, Vultuulf and her group gathering back at
the unofficial meeting place, awaiting instruction and hoping the next task might be easier.
Instead, it was more ships to pull, smaller longboats and other attachments to the main
ships. These still held cargo, so one rex from the group was assigned to swim behind the boats,
helping to keep them even - and rescuing any wayward packages, should they happen to take a
tumble.
Vultuulf was assigned to take the task of swimming. Oh Elders…
She hated the way water felt in her fur; even if it was minimal compared to some Rexals’
lavish hairstyles, it still felt like it dragged her down and made her heavier than usual.
Regardless, she padded into the water and pressed her forehead against the hull of the
raft. It was easy going at first; with the rain beating down and simply swimming along behind
the raft - then they hit the land, Vultuulf ’s feet catching on the lip of the start of shore.
The raft’s bow hits too, bumping the sandbar and swaying side to side for a moment
before it steadies, packages sliding dangerously to port and starboard before their brown paper
wrapping settles, sitting quietly once again.
Sand scrapes underfoot, scratching the hull and leaving shards of roosting barnacles on
the beach, shed from the raft as it is pulled fully ashore. Vultuulf pants, head hanging down and
resting on the aft of the small boat. The next fleet of rafts await, calling to the shore with
wooden creaks as they bob like unbothered seabirds on the rough waves.
The rexal shivers, cold wind picking up and cutting through her entire body. Even the
keratinous armor on her throat felt cold to the touch, the rounded edges almost like sharp
knives as she trod to the next launching point. The team moved in huddles from boat to boat;
crowded together like they could defeat the wind in a Roman platoon, though it was futile at
holding in any warmth - the boats continued down the shore, flocked together and drifting;
how could they ever rescue them all?
